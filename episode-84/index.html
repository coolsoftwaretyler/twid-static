Racism, TSA, and Science [#84]

17/02/19 at 12.11pm   /   by thisweekindrugs   /   0 Comment

Podcast: Play in new window | Download | Embed

Subscribe: Android | RSS

For this hiatus episode, Rachelle joins Sam to discuss the biggest stories and forecast upcoming events in drug policy.

News Updates:

  • House Bill Examines Racist Drug War
    • Nationally, African Americans are nearly four times more likely to be arrested for marijuana possession than their white neighbors.
  • TSA employees arrested for cocaine smuggling operation in Puerto Rico
  • Scientists in Canada have genetically-engineered cocaine-resistant mice
  • Secondhand smoke isn’t as dangerous as we once thought

Headlines:

  • The Trump administration issued a memo this week, proposing the elimination of several federal programs, including the Office of National Drug Control Policy.
  • Duterte targets Philippine children in bid to widen drug war
  • Alt-right website tries to weed out Jews from drug reform
  • Ganjapreneur Kayvan Khalatbari Files to Run for Mayor — and Isn’t Blowing Smoke
  • In New York, a judge ordered drug-using mother to not get pregnant until she regained legal custody of a previous child.

Forecast:

  • 2/21: National Day of Action on the Overdose Crisis in Canada
  • 2/28: “Know Your Rights; Safe and Legal Interactions with Law Enforcement” hosted by the Cannabis Cultural Association
